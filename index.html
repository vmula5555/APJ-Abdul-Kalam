Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kala was the "Missile Man of India"

Biography:

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (5 October 1931–27 July 2015) was an Indian aeronautical scientist and statesman who served as India's 11th president from 2002 to 2007. He was raised in a Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He was a well-known aeronautical scientist. He was key in India's civilian space program and military missile development. His contributions to developing ballistic missile and launch vehicle technologies garnered him significant recognition. Known for his humble demeanor and inspirational vision, he continued to inspire millions with his writings and lectures until his death on July 27, 2015.

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true."

Achievements:

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam made important contributions to India's space and missile programs, particularly in creating the country's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), successfully launching the Rohini satellite in 1980. He also contributed significantly to developing the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which improved India's defense capability. During his presidency of India from 2002 to 2007, he was praised for encouraging education and inspiring youth. His exploits garnered countless accolades, including India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna.

"Excellence is a continuous process and not an accident"​
